This is a work-in-progress list of educational resources on floating-point representations and other computer numerics topics.

Papers and Posts

  • "What Every Computer Scientist Should Know About Floating-point Arithmetic" by David Goldberg (1991)
  • "Do Developers Understand IEEE Floating Point" by Peter Dinda and Conor Hetland (2018)
  • "Floating Point Visually Explained" by Fabien Sanglard (2017)
  • IEEE 754-2019 (latest IEEE floating-point standard)
  • "Floating-point arithmetic" article on Wikipedia

Course Materials

  • "IEEE Format for Binary Floating Point Numbers" POGIL activity by Pam Smallwood (2023)
  • "Real Numbers in Binary", a section in the "Dive Into Systems" online systems textbook by Suzanne J. Matthews, Tia Newhall, and Kevin C. Webb (2022)
  • "Floating Point" flipped-classroom lecture videos by Mike Lam (2020) w/ accompanying slides based on content from CS:APP3e textbook by Randal E. Bryant and David R. O'Hallaron (2015)
  • "Reproducibility in Parallel Computing using Floating-Point Arithmetic" "peachy assignment" by Ganesh Gopalakrishnan (2018)

  • "Floating Point Numbers Part 1 (FP vs Fixed)" by Computerphile w/ Steven Bagley (2019)

  • "Visualizing the Floating Point Format" by Akash Murthy (2022)
  • "What Every 21st Century Computer Scientist Should Know About Floating-Point Arithmetic" workshop talk by Bill Zorn (2022)
  • "Z-Fighting" video about floating-point artifacts in 3d rendering by Jamie King (2012)
